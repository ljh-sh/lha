## Why both channels exist

§3 of the ORIGINAL LICENSE names author contact paths that are
no longer reachable in 2026:

- `y.tagawa@Nikkei-mix` (Y. Tagawa) — defunct since early 2000s
  per upstream `README.md` commentary
- `oki@netbsd.org` (M. Oki) — unverified since ~2015
- `watazaki@shimadzu.co.jp` (N. Watazaki) — institutional
  email; assumed retired
- `tsugio@muc.biglobe.ne.jp` (T. Okamoto) — last verified
  early 2000s

This distribution is the working substitute. The Debian
copyright record at
[`https://sources.debian.org/copyright/license/lha/1.14i-10.3/`](https://sources.debian.org/copyright/license/lha/1.14i-10.3/)
documents this author-contact EOL.

Providing two channels — private email and public issue — gives
every potential claimant a route that suits their comfort level,
while keeping a public record of any claim that prefers transparency.

## Out-of-scope: patents

This release does not create or assert any patent licence. The
LHa format itself is unencumbered (no licensing fees or royalties
required to use `.lzh`). The Stac Electronics LZS patent
US 5,016,009 expired **2009-07-14** due to non-payment of
maintenance fees; the LZ77 core patent US 4,558,302 expired
**2002**; LZARI / LZHUF are academic, no known patents. As of
2026, no in-force patent reads on LHa decoding or distribution.

For patent-specific concerns, contact via Channel 1 with the
patent number and specific implementation claimed to infringe.
